Your Story Starts Here

Nexdo Adventours: Trusted Tour Company in Uganda — Your Story Starts Here

Travel is not about ticking places off a list. It's about the sunrise over Lake Bunyonyi that made you go quiet. The gorilla that locked eyes with you in Bwindi. The windows-down playlist, the red dust on your shoes, the stories you'll tell for years.

That's the kind of travel we believe in. That's why we started Nexdo Adventours — a Kampala-based tour and travel company built to show you the real Uganda.

How It All Started — Easter 2021: Four friends – Gerald, Joy, Brian, and Smayah. One 4x4. A crazy idea: "What if travel in Uganda felt personal again?" We drove to Jinja, got lost twice, ate the best rolex by the roadside, and ended with a sunset boat cruise we didn't even book. No rushed schedule. No hidden fees. Just heart and memories. When we got back, people kept asking: "Can you organize tours in Uganda for us?" That question became Nexdo Adventours.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Ugandan adventure

Best Time to Visit

Dry seasons from June to August and December to February offer the best wildlife viewing and trekking conditions.

What to Pack

Light clothing, sunscreen, insect repellent, a good pair of hiking boots, and a camera to capture memories.

Safety Tips

Always follow guide instructions, stay in groups during wildlife encounters, and keep valuables secure.

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

Why visit? Home to half the world's mountain gorillas, Bwindi is a UNESCO World Heritage site offering the most unforgettable wildlife encounter on earth.

  • Track mountain gorillas in their natural habitat
  • Explore ancient rainforest with over 120 mammal species
  • Guided nature walks to waterfalls and Batwa cave trails
  • Birdwatcher's paradise with 350+ bird species
  • Community encounters with the Batwa pygmy people

Queen Elizabeth NP

Why visit? Uganda's most popular national park boasts tree-climbing lions, Kazinga Channel boat cruises, and incredible biodiversity.

  • Game drives to see lions, elephants, buffaloes, and antelope
  • Boat cruise along the Kazinga Channel — hippos, crocs, birds
  • Tree-climbing lions of the Ishasha sector
  • Lake Katwe salt mines and flamingo watching
  • Mweya Peninsula panoramic views of the Rwenzoris

Jinja — Adventure Hub

Why visit? The source of the Nile River and Uganda's adrenaline capital, Jinja offers world-class adventure activities surrounded by stunning scenery.

  • White-water rafting on the Nile (Grade 3-5 rapids)
  • Bungee jumping and kayaking
  • Source of the Nile boat tour
  • Quad biking and horse riding along the riverbanks
  • Sunset cruises and riverside dining

Kampala — City of Seven Hills

Why visit? Uganda's vibrant capital blends modern energy with deep cultural roots — from bustling markets to historic sites and lively nightlife.

  • Visit the Uganda Museum and Kasubi Tombs (UNESCO)
  • Shop at Owino Market and craft villages
  • Explore the Ndere Cultural Centre for music & dance
  • Nightlife at Kabalagala and Acacia Avenue
  • Panoramic views from the Gaddafi Mosque and Kololo Hill

Divine Resort and Spa

Why visit? A premium relaxation getaway just outside Kampala. Whether for a daycation or a multi-night stay, it's perfect for couples retreats, group unwinding, or solo relaxation.

  • Swimming pool and spa treatments
  • Boat rides on the resort lake
  • Nature walks and flower swing
  • Archery and paintball target shooting
  • Live band performances and bonfire nights
  • Overnight stays in luxury rooms available

Lemala Wild Waters Lodge

Why visit? A stunning island lodge on the Nile with thrilling water adventures and luxury relaxation.

  • Return boat ride to the island
  • Swimming in natural Nile rock pools
  • 3-course lunch with wine
  • Sunset views over the Nile
  • Transport from Kampala in a luxury Landcruiser
  • Overnight packages available for 2+ days
